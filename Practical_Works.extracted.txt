PROJECT 1: IDENTIFICATION SYSTEM
PROJECT CONTEXT
The university currently hosts 15,500 students, 1,250 faculty members, and 800 administrative staff.
Objective: Build a simple identity management system for a university department. You need to store information about students, teachers, and staff. Each person gets a unique ID. You should be able to create, view, search, update, and deactivate identities. No authentication, no authorization, no complex workflows. Just pure identity management.

IDENTIFIER MANAGEMENT RULES
Unique Identifier Format
Each person must have a unique identifier in the format: [TYPE][YEAR][NUMBER]
Rule: The identifier never changes, even if the person changes status (e.g., a student becoming faculty retains their STUDENT account but will have a second FACULTY account).

USE CASE EXAMPLES
Case 1: New Student Enrollment
"We have 2000 new high school graduates arriving in September. I need to enter their information and generate their student ID cards before the start of the academic year."
Case 2: Status Change
"Dr. Smith was a faculty member and has just been appointed Department Head. I need to update their profile with new responsibilities without losing historical data."
Case 3: Searching for an Alumnus
"An employer requests a degree verification for Mr. Johnson (class of 2015). I need to quickly find his file even though he is no longer in the active directory."

POSSIBLE STATUSES
Pending → Initial creation, documents under verification
Active → Operational account
Suspended → Temporary block (incident, non-payment)
Inactive → No longer affiliated (graduated, resigned)
Archived → Account deleted, legal retention

ALLOWED TRANSITIONS
Pending → Active (after verification)
Active → Suspended (administrative decision)
Suspended → Active (resolution)
Active → Inactive (end of affiliation)
Inactive → Archived (after 5 years)
